5 years in New Jersey

Laura♣Clover

I loved living there!

I have so many reasons why.

Military Base Living is Awesome

Although my parents were in the Navy, my family was stationed on an Army base, Fort Dix, New Jersey.
I lived there from the age of 7 through 12; which is why I think I liked it so much.
The population of Fort Dix is around 7,000, and the entire livingvside of the base was desgined
to be as perfect as possible. They had playgrounds on every block, they were exactly the same,
but you could see one jungle gym if you stood on top of another one. the houses were exactly the same as well.
I still have dreams about it. There were always kids around my age though. I have friends that are all over the
United States now. Being a military base, it was so safe you could play roller hockey in the street
and stay out well past dark and not have a worry.

1-2 Hour Commute to: New York, Philadelphia, THE OCEAN

I think this is pretty self explianitory(sp?). I love the ocean so very much and I miss it a lot. The water is suprisingly cold on the
east coast, but it's managable(sp?) at least some of the year. Philadelphia is a little farther away, and i don't remember much about the city
but I do remember Hersey Park. I love candy so obviously the park is going to stick out in my memory, but the whole town (named Hersey)
was truly magical. They maintained thier old buildings so well, they are still standing beautifully today. The street lamps are shaped like
Herseys Kisses and the chocolate factory is right next to the park, so you can smell chocolate as you enter the park. New York was my favorite
city though. I have never been to a city like it, so many different people and things going on. It's amazing how you can stand in Times Square and
be around more people than you could ever be comfortable with.

Snow

We had to much snow in New Jersey. There was one year where a snow drift got so deep on base that all te dads packed it down and made
a tunnel system/maze all through it. I remember making igloos, snow forts with compartments for snow balls and having two or three snow
days in a row. There was a rule set in place, the school could add on a day at the end of the year for every snow day we got. After five days
though, the snow days were deemed "Acts of God" and the school could no longer add days to the end of the school year. You can imagine how excited
we were when we got more than five snow days. Which happened almost every year I lived there.
